PLATFORM SELFHOOD AND ALGORITHMIC LIFE

These creators focus on how people become characters, brands, metrics, or moral positions inside platform culture. Their shared terrain is the self under algorithmic pressure: identity as content, taste as performance, politics as feed logic, and media literacy as survival skill.

Contributors: @davidkylechoe, @didoriot, @musingsofacrouton, @kai_rehagen, @bubsonline, @itsvicchang, @aidanetcetera Source: Instagram Reels + captions
Cross-Cutting Concepts
The Self as Format
Online identity is shaped by repeatable formats: the take, the persona, the confession, the fit, the bit, the metric.
via @davidkylechoe, @didoriot, @bubsonline, @itsvicchang
Metric Capture
Views, rankings, engagement, and algorithmic measures begin to dictate how people understand reality and themselves.
via @aidanetcetera, @musingsofacrouton, @kai_rehagen
Media Literacy as Tactic
Understanding the frame becomes a way to resist manipulation rather than merely explain it.
via @aidanetcetera, @didoriot, @davidkylechoe
Performance of Authenticity
Platforms reward sincerity only after it has been formatted into recognizable signals.
via @bubsonline, @itsvicchang, @musingsofacrouton
Absurdity as Diagnosis
Brainrot, jokes, and strange platform rituals reveal serious structures of labor, politics, and attention.
via @aidanetcetera, @kai_rehagen, @didoriot

Points of Tension
Participation vs. refusal
Side A
Some creators use platform forms knowingly to critique them from inside.
Side B
Others imply that every use of the form deepens dependence on the system.
Irony vs. sincerity
Side A
Absurdity exposes hidden rules.
Side B
Too much irony can make critique indistinguishable from the feed it critiques.
