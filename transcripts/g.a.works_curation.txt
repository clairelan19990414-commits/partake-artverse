@g.a.works curation

    Core thesis:
    @g.a.works is a printmaking-centered fine artist who layers serigraphy with acrylic and found ephemera (cigarette wrappers, paper) on progressively industrial substrates — paper, then board, then steel — as a material argument about durability and weight. The work fuses vernacular American pop culture (Vegas Vic, pinball, roadside food) with global references (Japan, Arabic, Latin America) and explicit political and literary citation (Baldwin, John Brown, anti-ICE imagery). The iterative "study" format made public signals that process and accumulation are as much the subject as any single finished piece.

    Key ideas:
    - Serigraphy as primary artistic language — screenprint is both medium and meaning, not decoration
- Material escalation from paper to board to steel as embedded political/conceptual statement about permanence
- Cross-cultural vernacular collision: Japan, Arabic, Mexico City, American Southwest coexist without hierarchy
- Explicit political iconography woven into fine-art practice (anti-ICE, John Brown, Baldwin)
- Found/ephemeral material inclusion (cigarette wrappers, paper scraps) within otherwise archival works
- Public iteration as practice — 'paper studies' posted openly, treating process as finished content
- American roadside and pop culture iconography (Vegas Vic, pinball, food) elevated to philosophical subject
- Literary and philosophical citation as visual layer (Sartre, Shakespeare, Abramović, Waylon Jennings)
