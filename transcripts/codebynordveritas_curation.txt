@codebynordveritas curation

    Core thesis:
    CODE by Nord Veritas argues that culture — art, architecture, institutions, collections — is never merely aesthetic but always a mechanism for signaling and consolidating capital, status, and power. The channel's method is structural decoding: stripping the surface language of taste and access to reveal the systems underneath. Its implicit claim is that power operates most effectively when it becomes invisible, naturalized as culture.

    Key ideas:
    - Culture as capital signal — aesthetic choices in art, architecture, and taste encode status and power, not just preference
- The validation stack — value is constructed sequentially (gallery → institution → criticism → collector), making price feel inevitable
- Structural vs. visible power — real power operates through systems, authorities, and infrastructure rather than titles or public roles
- The museum as editor — institutions pre-configure what becomes possible to show through economic self-adaptation, not formal censorship
- Market timing over taste — the art world functions on positioning and signal convergence, not aesthetic discovery
- Demand construction — desire and cultural legitimacy are manufactured (Bernays, Guggenheim, Simchowitz), not organically discovered
- Architecture as authority — built space (temples, banks, museums, highways) has always been used to materialize and transmit power
- Signal assembly — an artist's market acceleration results from a convergence of fair momentum, institutional validation, and collector signaling, not the work alone
