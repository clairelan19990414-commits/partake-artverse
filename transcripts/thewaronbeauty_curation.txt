@thewaronbeauty curation

    Core thesis:
    The account argues that Western civilization has waged a systematic war against beauty by abandoning Christianity and traditional values — and that ugliness is not aesthetic but moral and spiritual. Beauty is treated as a transcendental coequal with truth and goodness, meaning its decline is inseparable from secularism, consumerism, and Modernism. The path back runs through faith, particularly Catholic revival, classical philosophy, and a rejection of mass-produced, AI-generated, and ideologically motivated ugliness.

    Key ideas:
    - Beauty as transcendental — coequal with truth and goodness, not merely aesthetic preference
- Catholic/Christian faith as the generative source of Western beauty; revival of faith = revival of beauty
- Modernism as ideological assault — figures like Adolf Loos explicitly criminalizing ornament and tradition
- Consumerism and de-sacralization — secular materialism appropriates beauty's surface while gutting its metaphysical foundation
- Ruskin's framework — craft as moral act, the grotesque as either creative/faithful or base/vulgar, simplicity as architectural arrogance
- Classical philosophy (Plato, Aristotle) — ugliness causes psychological damage; beauty is a social and civic necessity
- AI and technology as existential threat to authentic human creativity in art, music, and architecture
- Post-political cultural realignment — Gen Z and others transcending Left/Right in a return to beauty, faith, and quality
