    Thesis:
    CODE by Nord Veritas argues that culture — art, architecture, institutions, collections — is never merely aesthetic but always a mechanism for signaling and consolidating capital, status, and power. The channel's method is structural decoding: stripping the surface language of taste and access to reveal the systems underneath. Its implicit claim is that power operates most effectively when it becomes invisible, naturalized as culture.

    Key concepts:
    - Culture as capital signal: Aesthetic choices in art, architecture, and taste encode status and power rather than mere preference — culture is infrastructure for class reproduction. (via DWJnHp7DQne, DXg5mSwDSEP)
- The validation stack: Market value is constructed sequentially through layered institutional endorsements — fair momentum, museum acquisition, critical attention, collector signal — making price feel inevitable rather than manufactured. (via DXZ5SppDbGn)
- Invisible order: When explicit classification dissolves (as in the LACMA redesign), hierarchy doesn't disappear — it becomes ambient and harder to contest, still determining what you see. (via DXUrSutDXuk)
- Demand construction: Desire and cultural legitimacy are manufactured through meaning-attachment (Bernays model), not organically discovered — wanting is engineered before the product is sold. (via DWqnWYAjUSX)
- Finance as architecture: Structural financial interventions (Rohatyn's NYC fiscal redesign) function like built space: they reorganize the conditions of possibility rather than solving surface problems. (via DWZVxnEjVK0)
- Power transmission through proximity: Durable power moves through methods and systems, not individuals — figures fade but their operational logics persist, transmitted quietly through networks rather than taught publicly. (via DWjlbKFDfVp)
- Permission as infrastructure: The AI-in-art debate is not about aesthetics or ethics but about which systems — markets, institutions, gatekeepers — hold authority to decide what counts as legitimate cultural production. (via DXXNPfODXMO)
- Historical continuity of the art market: The art world did not become financialized recently — Renaissance commissions already specified pigment grades, payment phases, and reputational signaling; only the language has changed. (via DXg5mSwDSEP)
