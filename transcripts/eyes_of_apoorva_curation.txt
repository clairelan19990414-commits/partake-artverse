@eyes_of_apoorva curation

    Core thesis:
    @eyes_of_apoorva excavates the hidden histories behind visual and cultural conventions — why things look the way they do, who decided that, and what it reveals about power, bias, and collective imagination. The creator traces influence chains across anime, film, design, and advertising to show that aesthetic "defaults" are almost never neutral. This analytical lens is woven through a personal visual diary of travel, poetry, and everyday beauty, grounding the intellectual in the felt.

    Key ideas:
    - Corporate capture of cultural imagery — single companies quietly standardize how billions picture gods, Santa, skin tones (Kodak), and holiday icons
- Color as ideological code — film color grading encodes political meaning (yellow Mexico, blue Russia) rather than pure aesthetics
- Cross-medium influence chains — real-world figures and art movements (a Swedish actor, Akira, Berserk) propagate across anime, games, and Hollywood for decades
- Design bias embedded in standards — ostensibly neutral defaults (photography calibration cards, font choices, ad liquid color) carry racial, gender, or class assumptions
- Narrative tropes as cultural infrastructure — damsel-in-distress, evil empire, plot twist, and apocalypse fantasy are recurring structural grammars that shape collective imagination
- Archaeology of symbols — everyday icons (heart shape, pink=girl, Kubrick Stare) have surprising, often arbitrary origins that harden into perceived natural law
- Personal visual diary as cultural method — travel, poetry, and found-beauty shots anchor analytical posts in lived sensory experience rather than pure lecture
