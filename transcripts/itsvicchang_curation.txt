    Core thesis:
    Vic Chang spots micro-trend signals (search volume spikes, Reddit communities, shifting cultural attitudes) and translates them into concrete brand or product design opportunities. He frames consumer behavior — status signaling, lifestyle aspiration, category white space — as the raw material for startup ideas. AI-accelerated prototyping is a recurring throughline: he treats rapid visual mockups as a core part of the ideation process, not just presentation.

    Key ideas:
    - Trend-to-brand pipeline: search volume spikes and subreddit activity as early signals for unmet product demand
- White space aestheticization: commoditized categories (litter boxes, paper plates, cat furniture) have room for a premium design layer
- Lifestyle signaling: products function as identity markers — luxury candles, NA cocktails, and clean energy drinks are status objects as much as functional goods
- Behavioral monetization redesign: rethinking incentive structures at the product level (pay-per-match vs. pay-per-swipe) to align user and business outcomes
- AI as rapid prototyping infrastructure: using generative image tools to collapse the distance between idea and visual pitch
- Subcultural design ahead of its time: avant-garde aesthetics that initially repel eventually become canonical (Y-3 Beast Pack as case study)
- Gender/identity deconstruction as brand philosophy: 'gender-ful' as a framework that expands market rather than narrowing to a niche
- Environmental psychology in retail: controlling pace of decision-making through sensory design (music tempo, spatial cues)
