@bjornd.al notes

    Thesis:
    @bjornd.al is a Minneapolis-based multi-hyphenate (musician, graphic designer, writer, video essayist) who uses absurdist humor as the connective tissue across wildly disparate creative output. His primary vehicle is "Esoteric Meme Breakdown," a video series that uses internet memes as entry points into obscure history, conspiracy lore, and art criticism. Simultaneously, he documents a dense local DIY scene through band promotion, self-published chapbooks, invented holidays, and hand-made merchandise — all delivered with a deadpan, self-aware voice.

    Positions:
    - Memes are legitimate intellectual vehicles — the absurd format earns its depth rather than undercutting it.
- Working around limitations is a path to mastery: Irving Berlin's inability to play outside F-sharp led him to commission transposing pianos and become one of the most prolific songwriters in American history.
- Lost or suppressed knowledge (Segato's petrification method, destroyed upon his death) is worth investigating and mourning — these gaps in the historical record are genuinely tragic.
- Community infrastructure — invented holidays, hand-made licenses, mascots — can be built through humor without sacrificing sincerity; the bit and the real thing coexist.
- Self-deprecating irony ('I am going to become an influencer now') is fully compatible with serious, sustained craft across multiple disciplines.
