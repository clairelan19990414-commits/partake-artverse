@andreyazizov notes

    Thesis:
    @andreyazizov treats commercial graphic design as a tactile, authored practice: pop campaigns, brand graphics, and personal music releases become more distinctive when they carry scanned material, distressed texture, hand-made type, and visible process. The recurring argument is that design gains emotional specificity when it borrows from physical residue -- paper, trash, dirt, lipstick, vintage print, and archival fragments -- rather than staying cleanly digital.

    Key concepts:
    - Tactile Graphic Authorship: A design approach that gives digital graphics presence by grounding them in hand-scanned paper, trash, dirt, lipstick, tape, stickers, and other physical residues. (via DWrm9Mxgcyl, DRftrPIDjgc, DL5eSPWxJqQ)
- Texture as Realism: Transparent paper, grit, and distressed scans are framed as subtle finishing tools that make flat design feel situated and alive. (via DW7BbYTAce-, DWrm9Mxgcyl, DRhsy6AjqYa)
- Commercial Credits as Cultural Map: Long credit lists for Vogue, Sabrina Carpenter, Redken, Old Navy, KFC, and record labels make design visible as a distributed production network. (via C7654cfxTmM, C693GCgRnwq, DQKIM-6jsjU)
- Asset Packs as Pedagogy: Selling or giving away texture packs turns process into a shared tool kit, teaching followers to reproduce a sensibility through source material rather than presets alone. (via DRiUMRODmpz, DRftrPIDjgc, DMv4roPx6bi)
- Music and Design as One Practice: Song releases, cover art, video stills, and fonts are presented as one integrated creative system rather than separate careers. (via DLWZCESsdaU, DMxmWUsx1qQ, DLXvR4tx6d1)
- Political Texture: Even visual posts occasionally turn texture and remix into a civic statement, linking immigration, wealth, and everyday labor to graphic culture. (via DT8O4vcDjsg, DK7kycGRv2D)

    Positions:
    - Design becomes more memorable when it carries the evidence of material contact, not only digital polish.
- Commercial graphics are collaborative cultural artifacts, and crediting the production network is part of the work.
- Scanned trash, dirt, and sheer paper can be elevated into useful design language rather than treated as noise.
- Personal creative output and client work can feed the same visual vocabulary when process is consistent.
