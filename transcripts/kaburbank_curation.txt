@kaburbank curation

    Core thesis:
    Jessica Burbank is a progressive journalist and political commentator who investigates the intersection of surveillance capitalism, war profiteering, and concentrated elite power — arguing that both parties have failed ordinary people as tech companies and capital syndicates increasingly operate outside democratic accountability. She blends on-the-ground investigative reporting (FOIA requests, city council appearances) with a broader theoretical framework about how global power has been restructured around capital rather than nation-states. Her project is equal parts exposure and movement-building: naming the machinery and then organizing readers to fight it.

    Key ideas:
    - Mass surveillance infrastructure: Flock Safety's national license-plate-reader network as a privatized police state sold city-by-city
- Syndicates of Capital: global elites operating as organized capital blocs that bypass and hollow out the nation-state entirely
- War profiteering loop: the same tech/defense ecosystem profits from building weapons, deploying them, and reconstructing the damage
- FOIA-driven citizen journalism: using public records requests and municipal meetings as the lever for exposing corporate-government collusion
- Progressive digital strategy: the left needs algorithmic fluency to compete with right-wing media machinery on platforms like TikTok
- Democratic Party reform from the voter base up: guaranteed healthcare, union jobs, and anti-war spending as the actual winning platform
- Pop-culture as political education: using Andor, MMT debates, and meme-format commentary to make structural critique legible to non-ideological audiences
