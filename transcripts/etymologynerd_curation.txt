@etymologynerd curation

    Core thesis:
    @etymologynerd creates infographic-based content that traces the word origins of everyday objects, places, and concepts — showing how language acts as a compressed archive of human history, culture, and migration. The creator's thesis is that etymology is universally accessible and endlessly surprising: virtually any domain (food, geography, science, music) reveals hidden linguistic patterns once you look. Most recently, this framework has been extended into a book arguing that social media algorithms are the newest force reshaping how language evolves.

    Key ideas:
    - Etymology as hidden history — words carry compressed records of culture, migration, and discovery
- Toponym patterns — place names (cities, neighborhoods, regions) encode colonial, indigenous, and immigrant history
- Scientific naming conventions — taxonomy, anatomy, geology, and medicine follow systematic Latin/Greek construction rules
- Brand and cultural naming — artist names, beer labels, and art movements reflect the era and values that coined them
- Algospeak — internet algorithms and platform moderation are now an active driver of new vocabulary formation
- Cross-domain universality — the same etymological forces (metaphor, borrowing, eponymy) operate identically across unrelated fields
- Visual/spatial etymology mapping — presenting word origins as infographics and maps makes abstract linguistic history legible at a glance
