@didoriot notes

    Thesis:
    @didoriot applies marketing and cultural critique to internet trends, influencer aesthetics, and consumer behavior — treating brand decisions, platform dynamics, and online identity as legitimate objects of analysis. The creator operates from inside the culture they critique: a salaried professional who also writes on Substack, crosses between TikTok and Instagram, and is openly figuring out the creator economy in real time. The throughline is the tension between aspiration and reality — in careers, in consumption, in how people perform themselves online.

    Key concepts:
    - Goop-ification / conversation piece products: Brands releasing products that function as cultural signals rather than functional goods — earnestly marketed as wellness, but really purchased to virtue-signal membership in an increasingly niche audience. (via DRU2xzpiS4w)
- Parasocial management of the Instagram boyfriend: Influencers strategically calibrate partner visibility to protect audience parasocial attachment; the only socially acceptable way to feature a boyfriend online is through complete gender role reversal — putting him behind the camera. (via DU1e1q7iVMb)
- Attention span as future capital: AI advancement will bifurcate the workforce into a 'thinking class' that can manage its own attention and cognition versus those who cannot — positioning attention span as the new EQ/IQ differentiator. (via DSQefheCSD4)
- Gen Alpha marketing overreach: Brands crossing unspoken ethical boundaries by marketing adult wellness products to children, coinciding with legislative responses like Australia's under-16 social media ban. (via DSQefheCSD4)
- 'You can just do things' / free will discourse: A Gen Z cultural meme about reclaiming agency as a response to decision paralysis caused by infinite online options — the generational successor to the millennial 'adulting' frame. (via DTlGB7EEX9p)
- Dream job displacement and the Substack era: Institutional cultural authority (Vogue, print media) has collapsed into the creator economy; the new aspirational 'cool girl' career is Substack newsletter writer — fashionable, smart, and immune to layoffs. (via DUJDVG0jgcq)
- Gendered etiquette curriculum online: Social media replicates centuries of formal femininity instruction by converting individual women (Carolyn Bessette-Kennedy, 'that girl') into gendered templates — diluting the original essence that made them compelling, and making girls lonelier. (via DWHfsAcCWqI)
- Provocation marketing / controversy as free engagement: Large multinationals deliberately court backlash (AI Super Bowl ads, boycotted brand Coachella trips) because hate comments are still engagement and negative press clarifies their core consumer against everyone else. (via DXPI238jjV4)
