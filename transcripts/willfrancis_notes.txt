    Thesis:
    Will Francis is a practical AI educator who demystifies new tools, models, and workflows for everyday creators and marketers. His content is built around actionable tutorials — showing exactly how to use the latest AI releases to save time, make content, and build things. A secondary thread runs through his work around AI literacy: helping audiences recognise AI-generated text, video, and media so they're not fooled by it.

    Key concepts:
    - Prompt injection: A security attack where hidden commands are embedded in web content (e.g. near-invisible text) to hijack an AI browser agent into performing unauthorized actions like accessing accounts or exfiltrating data. (via DQKCHUIDMnz)
- Vibe coding / no-code AI building: Using AI scaffolding tools like Bolt to build fully functional apps from a plain-English prompt, without writing or touching a single line of code. (via DPrQumhAR0M)
- Agentic AI: AI systems that autonomously plan and execute multi-step tasks across apps and the web — going beyond single-turn chat to complete whole projects from one prompt. (via DU_XMyDDqiM)
- AI writing detection: A set of learnable signals — em dashes, 'puffery,' fake balance structures, hallmark words like 'delve,' curly quotes, fabricated citations — that reliably distinguish AI-generated prose from human writing. (via DW4fkQPDh6R)
- Claude Skills (portable AI identity): Custom, reusable instruction profiles trained on brand assets (slides, fonts, colours) and saved inside Claude, so the model applies consistent style and rules across future sessions without re-prompting. (via DXKY8s5Dr4F)
- AI policy lag: The structural gap between how fast AI capabilities grow and how slowly law and regulation can catch up — meaning only the companies that built the models can currently make reliable safety judgements about them. (via DVZb9Z1CF8p)
- AI slop and the creativity rebound: The thesis that formulaic AI-generated content is forcing human creators toward more experimental, rule-breaking work — mirroring how photography once pushed painters away from realism toward Impressionism and Dadaism. (via DWmmBSiDmQp)
