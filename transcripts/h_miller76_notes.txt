    Thesis:
    Harry Miller is a New York-based poet and personal trainer who fuses physical discipline, spoken word, and spiritual philosophy into a unified practice of self-overcoming. His central argument is that wisdom, poetry, and bodily strength are all forms of warfare — tools for confronting inner demons, societal indifference, and existential despair. He channels personal experience with depression and suicide into a public advocacy posture, positioning resilience as prophetic and the struggle itself as the path to victory.

    Key concepts:
    - Wisdom as Warfare: Wisdom, poetry, and physical strength are not passive virtues but active weapons — instruments for combating inner darkness and existential despair. (via DUJVdNBkfXB, DTLo2SPkXMC)
- Hell as the Battlefield: Suffering and inner darkness must be descended into rather than avoided, because demons can only be slain where they live — making hardship itself the site of victory. (via DXFaF3nEVlx)
- Prophetic Identity: The truth-teller is always dismissed by contemporaries and vindicated only later — a posture Miller applies to mental health advocates, weeping visionaries, and himself. (via DW4iHQKEf09)
- Mental Health Advocacy as Public Mission: Personal experience with depression and suicide is transformed into prophetic public testimony — a moral obligation to speak, witness, and prevent further loss. (via DJrVbdDxNfI, C0epuIxuAJ-)
- Poetry as Violent Assault: Poetry is conceived not as gentle expression but as a forceful act — an assault on unknown forces, after Marinetti — that commands transformation. (via DTLo2SPkXMC)
- Sermon of the Earth: A communal spiritual practice framework combining nature, music, and spoken word as collective presence and grounding — positioned as an alternative sacred gathering. (via DXcCFChDUo1)
- Law of Conservation of Fuckery: A darkly humorous philosophical aphorism suggesting that chaos and difficulty are conserved — a coping posture that uses wit to metabolize suffering. (via DSk90FGEVVN)
- Physical Training as Spiritual Discipline: The gym is a site of the same self-overcoming practiced in poetry and philosophy — bodily strength and mental resilience are parallel, mutually reinforcing forms of warfare. (via DXcCFChDUo1, DTLo2SPkXMC)

    Positions:
    - "You came here to kill demons. Of course you have to go through Hell. Isn't that where demons live?" — suffering is not an obstacle to the mission but its necessary terrain.
- "Wisdom is Warfare." — the central aphorism condensing his entire philosophical framework into a confrontational imperative.
- "Those are dangerous days, when the comedians make the most sense, and the weeping are scoffed at" — a prophetic lament that truth-telling is dismissed while spectacle is rewarded.
- Poetry must be a violent assault on unknown forces — Miller endorses Marinetti's combative conception of art as force, not decoration.
- Personal experience with depression and suicide creates a public obligation to witness — resilience is not private achievement but prophetic testimony.
