@eugbrandstrat curation

    Core thesis:
    Eugene Healey argues that brands must identify and amplify existing cultural currents rather than attempt to manufacture culture themselves — functioning as patrons, not architects. He applies critical theory (Baudrillard, Lacan) to decode how late capitalism is restructuring desire, identity, and status, particularly as traditional aspirational levers (education, class mobility, luxury goods) collapse. The practical output is a strategic framework for brands navigating a post-aspiration, post-luxury, algorithmically-saturated consumer environment.

    Key ideas:
    - Brands as cultural patrons, not creators — brands must grant existing culture a platform and enable transference of cultural capital, not attempt to manufacture it
- Post-luxury behavioural status symbols — material luxury is algorithmically commodified; status now signals through behaviour (connected privacy, hyper-niche expertise, wasteful time, aspirational parenthood, quality sleep, community creation)
- Baudrillardian body commodification — as external systems (education, careers, class mobility) fail, the self and body become the final investable asset under capitalism
- Aspiration collapse in the k-shaped economy — brands cannot sell aspiration to Gen Z living a casino economy; strategic pivot required from identity signalling to utility and forward mobility
- Decentralised desire and the creator/celebrity distinction — creator culture has disaggregated the desire-formation function previously held by Hollywood; creators are not celebrities and should not be briefed as such
- Ontological collapse and brand mythology — the cultural mythologies underpinning Western brand equity are dissolving, leaving brands built on those beliefs structurally exposed
- Mediated selfhood and the Lacanian gaze — chronic online existence produces a performed self optimised for perception rather than authentic presence, with brand and commercial implications
- Brand coherence at infinite surface area — traditional guidelines fail when AI and distributed creation expand brand touchpoints infinitely; new infrastructure required to maintain coherence
